CONSTANTS vs ENTITIES — CRITICAL DISTINCTION
  • constants  → Proper nouns, named individuals, specific events/places.
                 MUST go in the constants array, NEVER in entities.
                 Use the camelCase form of the name as the ID.
                 Examples: Bonnie → id="bonnie", Lana Wilson → id="lanaWilson",
                           After Tiller → id="afterTiller", Harvard → id="harvard"
  • entities   → Quantified things: indefinite nouns ("a car"), universal ("all kids"),
                 definite generics ("the tree", "the region").
                 Use e1, e2, … as IDs.

ENTITY TYPE (entities only)
  • existential  → indefinite: "a car", "some bears", "the tree"
  • universal    → quantified over all: "all kids", "every student", "no bird"

FACT ARITY (implicit from len(args)):
  • 1-arg → unary predicate (type or property): Tall(e1), Queen(bonnie)
  • 2-arg → binary relation: Directed(lanaWilson, afterTiller), Likes(e1, e2)
  • 3-arg → ternary relation: Gave(bonnie, e1, e2)

DECOMPOSITION RULES — MANDATORY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
You MUST decompose complex noun phrases into their atomic components.
Each entity surface must be a SINGLE NOUN or COMPOUND NOUN — never a clause,
never a phrase containing verbs or prepositions.

PREPOSITIONAL PHRASES — separate entity + binary relation fact:
  "people in this club" → entity "people" + entity "club" + fact in(people, club)
  "members of the community" → entity "members" + entity "community" + fact of(members, community)
  VIOLATION: creating entity with surface "people in this club" — NEVER DO THIS

RELATIVE CLAUSES — decompose the clause into its own facts:
  "students who attend the school" → entity "students" + entity "school" + fact attend(students, school)
  "people who chaperone dances" → entity "people" + entity "dances" + fact chaperone(people, dances)
  VIOLATION: creating entity with surface "students who attend the school" — NEVER DO THIS

CONJUNCTION/DISJUNCTION — preserve each conjunct as a separate fact:
  "attend and are very engaged with school events" → fact attend(e1, e2) + fact very engaged with(e1, e2)
  "inactive and disinterested" → fact inactive(e1) + fact disinterested(e1)

NEGATION — use the negated field on facts, NEVER use "not" as a predicate:
  "are not students" → fact students(e1) with negated=true
  VIOLATION: fact with pred="not" — NEVER DO THIS

ADVERB + ADJECTIVE/VERB — keep adverbs attached to the predicate they modify:
  "very engaged with" → pred "very engaged with"
  "perform often" → pred "perform often in" or separate fact often(e1)

2. USE COMPOUND ANALYSIS — the "COMPOUND ANALYSIS" block (when present) shows

3. MACRO TEMPLATE — choose the single best-matching template:
   • universal_affirmative   (TYPE A):  All P are Q       → ∀x(P(x) → Q(x))

   • existential_affirmative (TYPE I):  Some P are Q      → ∃x(P(x) ∧ Q(x))
   • existential_negative    (TYPE O):  Some P are not Q  → ∃x(P(x) ∧ ¬Q(x))
   • ground_positive                    P(c) or P(c) ∧ Q(c)
   • ground_negative                    ¬P(c)
   • conditional                        A → B
   • biconditional                      A ↔ B
